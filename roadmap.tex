\section*{Minimal remaining steps for a complete proof}

\begin{enumerate}[label=\textbf{Step \arabic*.},leftmargin=3.5em]
  \item \textbf{Prove the perpendicular dissipation sign $\mathcal{D}_\perp\le 0$
    (Conjecture~B, most direct path).}
    Along the dilation path $r_t=p\boxplus_n q_t$, decompose the root velocity
    into a score-aligned component ($\alpha V_i/(n{-}1)$) and an
    orthogonal correction~$\varepsilon_i$.
    Show that the dissipation pairing from $\varepsilon$ is non-positive.
    This immediately gives repulsion monotonicity
    ($\Phi_n(r_t)$ non-increasing).

  \item \textbf{Prove $\Gamma^{(1)}(p)>0$ for general $n$.}
    This is proved for $n=3$ via palindromic positivity.
    For $n\ge 4$, try $n$-variable partial-fraction identities
    analogous to the $\Phi_n=2\mathcal{R}$ proof.
    Combined with Step~1, this gives $F''(0)>0$ and
    strong evidence for the pointwise dilation Stam.

  \item \textbf{Prove the pointwise dilation Stam $E(t)\ge 0$, or (weaker)
    the integrated Stam $\int_0^1 F'(t)\,dt\ge 1/\Phi_n(q)$.}
    The former is the strongest
    plausible statement. The latter suffices for the Stam inequality.
    Approach: use $E(0)=E'(0)=0$, $E''(0)>0$, and the fact that $E(t)/t^2$
    has a positive initial value and is controlled by the repulsion monotonicity.

  \item \textbf{Handle the boundary} (repeated roots):
  By the convention $1/\Phi_n:=0$ when $p$ has repeated roots
  and the continuity of $\boxplus_n$, Stam extends to the boundary;
  this step is already addressed in the main paper.
\end{enumerate}

\section*{Recommended next routes}

\begin{description}[leftmargin=2em,style=nextline]
  \item[Route B (Operator-coefficient monotone path) — most developed.]
    The dilation path provides a smooth, real-rooted interpolation
    $r_t=p\boxplus_n q_t$ from $p$ to $p\boxplus_n q$.
    \textbf{New results:}
    \begin{itemize}[nosep]
      \item The initial root acceleration is proportional to the score:
        $\ddot\lambda_i(0)=\frac{2\sigma^2(q)}{n-1}V_i(p)$ (Proposition, proven).
      \item A new functional $\Gamma^{(1)}(p)>0$ controls $F''(0)$;
        proved positive for $n=3$ via palindromic polynomial analysis.
      \item The dissipation decomposes as
        $\mathcal{D}=\mathcal{D}_\parallel+\mathcal{D}_\perp$,
        where the Hermite-aligned part $\mathcal{D}_\parallel>0$ always
        and the perpendicular correction $\mathcal{D}_\perp\le 0$
        never dominates ($3700+$ tests, zero violations).
      \item \textbf{Key conjecture:} the ``pointwise dilation Stam''
        $1/\Phi_n(r_t)\ge 1/\Phi_n(p)+t^2/\Phi_n(q)$
        holds for all $t\in[0,1]$ (zero violations in $3700+$ tests).
    \end{itemize}
    \textbf{Minimal remaining step:}
    prove $\mathcal{D}_\perp\le 0$
    (or directly prove repulsion monotonicity).

  \item[Route C (Variational/transport approach).]
    View the root measure $\mu_p=\frac{1}{n}\sum\delta_{\lambda_i}$
    as a discrete probability measure, and $\mathcal{R}$ as a
    Riesz-type energy $I_{-2}(\mu_p)$.
    The discriminant power inequality (Conjecture: $\mathcal{N}(p\boxplus_n q)\ge
    \mathcal{N}(p)+\mathcal{N}(q)$) survives $29{,}000+$ tests
    and is proved for $n\le 3$.

  \item[Hybrid approach.]
    Combine the Hermite flow bound (proved) with
    the Route~B velocity decomposition.
    The score-aligned component gives a Hermite-type bound;
    the perpendicular correction is always non-positive.
    Key step: prove the perpendicular correction is \emph{bounded}
    in a way that the net dissipation suffices for Stam.
\end{description}
